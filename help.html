Safe support guide

If you think an animal is injured, keep a safe distance and contact a trained wildlife rescue for advice. For your safety, avoid handling wildlife unless a professional tells you what to do.

If you find an injured animal

Do:

  • Stay calm and keep voices low.
  • Keep children and pets well back.
  • Note the location, time, and what you observed (e.g., limping, not moving, trapped).
  • Contact a wildlife rescue centre for guidance.

Only if a professional advises you to contain it:

  • Use thick gloves or a towel (avoid direct contact).
  • Place the animal in a ventilated box lined with a towel.

Young animals: when to leave them alone

Many young animals are left alone while a parent forages. Picking them up too quickly can do more harm than good.

Often normal:

A young bird on the ground (a fledgling) hopping around and calling ‚Äî parents may be nearby.

Get advice urgently if:

The animal is in immediate danger (road, pets), visibly injured, or cold and unresponsive.

Make your garden wildlife-friendly

  • Leave small gaps under fences so hedgehogs can travel between gardens.
  • Check long grass before mowing or strimming.
  • Add an escape ramp to ponds (a plank or textured stone).
  • Reduce pesticides to protect insects that birds and mammals rely on.
  • Put out fresh water in hot weather using a shallow dish.
  • Secure bins and remove litter to prevent entanglement.

Visitor etiquette

Recovery areas are sensitive. Please keep voices low, avoid flash photography, and never tap or knock on enclosures. Wildlife needs calm conditions to recover.
